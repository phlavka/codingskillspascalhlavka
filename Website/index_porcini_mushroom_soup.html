Porcini Mushroom Soup

Meal Description

This soup is made from a rich and flavorful base of sautéed onions, garlic, and fresh porcini mushrooms that are then simmered with broth and cream to create a smooth and creamy texture. The soup is finished with a touch of nutmeg and chopped fresh parsley for added depth of flavor and freshness. Porcini mushrooms are a meaty and earthy variety that add a distinct umami flavor to the soup, making it a hearty and satisfying meal on its own or a delicious starter for any dinner party.

Ingredients

  • 25 g dried porcini mushrooms
  • 1 handful of chestnuts (sweet chestnuts), pre-cooked
  • 3 shallots
  • 500 g potatoes
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 100 ml white wine
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp Worcester sauce (vegan variety, gluten-free if necessary)
  • Pepper, black, ground, Salt, 7 teaspoons olive oil, 1 teaspoon fresh rosemary
  • 2-3 dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 spring onion
  • ½ bunch of flat-leaf parsley

Instructions

  1. Pour 100 ml boiling water over the porcini mushrooms and leave to soak for 20 minutes. Remove the mushrooms from the soaking water, squeeze them out a little and set aside. Pour the water through a fine-mesh sieve to filter out any sand and collect the soaking water.
  2. Heat 3 tbsp. olive oil in a large saucepan and sauté the shallots and chestnuts in it. Add the potatoes, garlic and rosemary. Stir in the rest of the olive oil and fry for a few minutes.
  3. Now stir in the porcini mushrooms, then add the wine, the collected mushroom water and 1 l of water. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  4. I cook the soup without stock broth or many ingredients to bring out the fine, earthy taste of the porcini and chestnuts.
  5. Add the balsamic vinegar and the Worcester sauce. Simmer for ten minutes.
  6. Serve with dry tomatos.

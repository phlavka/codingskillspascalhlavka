Burgundy Roast

Meal Description

This classic French dish features a beef roast that is marinated in a mixture of red wine, herbs, and spices for several hours before being slow-cooked to tender perfection in the oven. The result is a rich and flavorful roast with a deep, velvety wine sauce that is perfect for a cozy winter dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2kg Roast beef
  • Pepper, Salt, Garlic,2 bay leafs, sunflower oil
  • 360g White onions
  • 260g carrots
  • 240g celery
  • 1 bottle burgundy red wine
  • 160g leek
  • 400ml beef soup
  • 60g tomato paste

Instructions

  1. Season the roast with pepper and place it in an appropriately sized freezer bag.
  2. For the marinade, peel or clean the onion, carrot, celery and garlic. Grate the onion, carrot and finely grate the onion, carrot and celery in a blender. Add the vegetables, the garlic clove, peppercorns and bay leaf around the meat. Pour the red wine over the roast close the bag and leave to cool for at least 1 hour.
  3. Remove the meat from the marinade, drain well and pat dry. Pour the marinade through a sieve, collect the liquid and pour some of it into the roasting syringe. Now inject the red wine marinade into the roast beef.
  4. Peel or clean the celery, carrot, onion and leek and cut into pieces. Salt the meat, heat the oil heat the oil in a roasting tin and fry the meat all over in the oil. sear the meat well in the oil and remove. Saut√© the prepared vegetables, stir in the tomato paste and fry briefly. Place the roast on top of the vegetables, add 2/3 of the marinade liquid, cover the roast and place it in the preheated oven at 150 degrees Celsius for 1.5-2 hours, repeatedly basting with the braising liquid and the rest of the marinade and beef stock.
  5. Remove the roast from the oven, wrap in foil and leave to rest for about 10 minutes. Pour the braising liquid through a fine sieve into a saucepan squeeze out the vegetables a little. Bring the sauce to the boil and reduce by about 1/3, thicken slightly if necessary and season again.
  6. Cut the roast into slices and serve with the sauce on plates.
  7. Tip: It goes very well with red cabbage and boiled potatoes.

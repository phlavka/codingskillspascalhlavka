Darkbeer Goulash

Meal Description

This Hungarian stew is made with chunks of beef that are seared and then slow-cooked in a rich, dark beer sauce with paprika, caraway seeds, and a touch of tomato paste. The result is a bold and flavorful goulash with a deep, savory taste that pairs perfectly with crusty bread or creamy mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1,5kg beef rump (Siroloin)
  • 1 pointed cabbage
  • 1kg carrots
  • 1 leek
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 4 shallots
  • 4tbsp tomato paste
  • 3tbsp flour
  • 1/2L of dark beer (Chefs choice: Harderner from Munich)
  • ½ L of beef soup
  • Brown bread
  • 7 juniper berries, 3 bay leaves, salt, pepper

Instructions

  1. First roughly chop the vegetables and sauté them. Set aside after about 4 minutes. Fry the meat in the same pan until just as hot. Put the meat aside as well.
  2. Mix the gravy with the tomato paste and add the flour, stir well with a whisk but do not let it burn. Deglaze peu à peu with the soup and the beer.
  3. Place the vegetables and meat on a baking tray or in a casserole dish. Pour the sauce through a sieve into the casserole dish. Cover and simmer at 200 degrees Celsius for 2 hours.
  4. Meanwhile, cut the bread into cubes. Remove the lid from the baking dish 15 minutes before the end and leave to cook uncovered. Fry the bread in a pan with butter.
  5. Serve the goulash with the bread cubes and a cold beer.

Brussels Sprout Soup

Meal Description

This soup is made from a blend of sautéed Brussels sprouts, onions, garlic, and vegetable broth that is then pureed to create a smooth and creamy texture. The soup is finished with a touch of cream, nutmeg, and a sprinkle of crispy bacon for added flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • 500 g Brussels sprouts
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp vegetable stock (instant)
  • Salt, Pepper
  • ½ bunch of flat-leaf parsley
  • ½ bunch of basil
  • 100 g spelt baguette (or wholemeal baguette)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • ½ bunch chives
  • 100 g cheddar cheese (piece)
  • 100 g whipped cream
  • 150 g sliced bacon

Instructions

  1. Clean and wash the brussels sprouts, remove a few leaves and set aside. Cut the rest in half. Peel and finely chop the onion and garlic. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large saucepan. Sauté the onion, garlic and Brussels sprout halves in it. Deglaze with 3⁄4 l water. Stir in stock and bring to the boil. Cover and simmer for approx 15 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, blanch the Brussels sprouts in boiling salted water for approx. 2 minutes. Drain, rinse with cold water and leave to drain.
  3. For the croutons, wash the herbs, shake dry and pluck the leaves. Mix the herbs and 100 ml oil in a high mixing bowl and purée with a hand blender. Season with salt. Cut the bread into approx. 2 cm cubes. Melt the butter in a pan. Fry the bread cubes in it until crispy all over. Add approx. 1⁄3 of the herb oil and toss the croutons briefly in it. Remove from the heat.
  4. Grate the cheese. Pour the cream into the soup and puree everything finely with a hand blender. Add approx. 2⁄3 of the cheese and melt in the hot soup while stirring. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the soup with the cabbage leaves, croutons, remaining cheese and fried bacon slices. Drizzle with a little herb oil. Serve the rest with it.
